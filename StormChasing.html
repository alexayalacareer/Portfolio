Overview

Storm chasing is a mix of preparation and adaptability. It starts long before leaving home by studying weather models, tracking risk areas, and building a plan with multiple backup routes. In the field, conditions can change quickly, so the focus is staying calm, making smart decisions, and being ready to pivot when the storm evolves.

For me, it is about capturing powerful weather with intention, not just ‚Äúseeing a storm.‚Äù Each trip builds real-world experience in pattern recognition, situational awareness, and documenting a fast-changing environment.

Forecasting and Planning

  • Monitor outlooks and model guidance to identify target regions
  • Plan routes with multiple options for changing storm motion
  • Track radar trends and updates throughout the day
  • Balance opportunity with safety and visibility

Field Workflow

In the field, the goal is to stay ahead of the storm while maintaining safe positioning and a clear escape route. I track storm structure, motion, and visibility, then decide whether to hold position for photography or reposition to stay aligned with the best perspective.

  • Evaluate storm structure and lighting for photography
  • Maintain situational awareness and avoid risky road decisions
  • Capture stills and short clips with a focus on composition and clarity
  • Log locations and notes to improve decision-making on future chases

Outcome and Takeaways

Storm chasing has taught me how to think clearly under uncertainty, stay patient when outcomes are unknown, and make strong decisions with incomplete information. It blends planning with real-world adaptability, and it has strengthened my ability to observe complex systems, recognize patterns, and document fast-moving events with discipline.
